# Training Feedback — Day 9

**Round:** 9,000 pairs · ppl **7.05** · retention **13.18 (+16.2%)** · judge **1.54** · $0.174

> The deciding round. Day 7 jumped to 1.60, Day 8 held. Was it signal?

---

## Finding 1 — The bump COLLAPSED: judge 1.60 → 1.54 (−0.06)

**Answer: it was noise.** The Day-7/8 excursion to 1.60 gave back exactly what it gained (−0.06,
mirroring the +0.06), returning to the same ~1.54 the curve has occupied since Day 6. There was no
late climb.

**What we actually learned — and it's genuinely valuable:** we have now measured **the judge's noise
band**. Across eight rounds with the method frozen and only data growing, the score has wandered:

```
1.50 · 1.46 · 1.50 · 1.52 · 1.54 · 1.60 · 1.60 · 1.54
        └──────────── range ≈ ±0.07 around ~1.53 ────────────┘
```

**Implication:** any future judge movement **smaller than ~±0.07 on this 50-question set is not
interpretable.** This retroactively validates the Day-4 stop rule (±0.05 was, if anything, slightly too
tight — but the direction was right) and means the entire Days 2–10 range is **one flat line with
noise**, not a series of small gains.

## Finding 2 — This is why the 10× run was worth the $3

Had the study stopped at Day 8, the honest write-up would have had to say *"quality may improve beyond
7k pairs — unresolved."* Day 9 closes that door cleanly. **A negative result is only as strong as the
alternatives you eliminated**, and Days 7–9 eliminated the most plausible one.

## Finding 3 — Forgetting: monotonic to the end

Retention **13.10 → 13.18 (+16.2%)**. Nine rounds, nine increases, zero reversals. Meanwhile ppl
**7.12 → 7.05** (nine rounds, nine improvements). The two "responsive" metrics remain the two that
don't measure what we care about.
