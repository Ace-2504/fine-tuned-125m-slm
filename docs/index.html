04The honest finding

Perplexity is not quality. Doubling the data nudged the judge from 1.32 to 1.50 and improved some answers (severability flipped from wrong to essentially correct) — but recall questions still degenerate.

Q · What does it mean for a contract clause to be severable?
Day 1: "…is not severable, and the parties are bound by the terms of the contract." → Day 2: "The contract clause is severable from the other provisions of the contract." ✓ closer
Q · What is the standard of proof in a civil lawsuit?
Day 2: "…is not a standard of proof, but is a standard of proof that is applied to a case." (still circular)
Q · Summarize what a 10-K annual report contains.
Day 2: "Summarize the report to include a 10-K annual report." (still echoes the prompt)

The slow climb matches the known limitation: ~40% of training is closed-book QA, which asks a 125M model to recall long-tail facts from a single exposure — the hardest thing to teach via SFT. Grounded (context-in-prompt) tasks are learnable; recall is not. This is logged as feedback for a possible v2.

06Method

  • Base: LlamaForCausalLM, 12L / 768d / 12h, 125.8M params, vocab 16,384, context 1,024, tied embeddings — pretrained from scratch on 2.04B tokens (val ppl 11.35).
  • Teacher: gemini-3.1-flash-lite generates grounded QnA whose answers are stated only in the source passage; free tier, $0.
  • Training: full fine-tune, 3 epochs, batch 16, LR 3e-5→3e-6 cosine, AdamW, bf16, assistant-only loss masking, seed 1337.
  • Design: each day re-initialises from the base checkpoint on the cumulative dataset — the only variable is dataset size, so the curve isolates the effect of data.
  • Eval: SFT-eval perplexity (task fit) · retention perplexity on pretraining val (forgetting) · Gemini-as-judge 1–5 (answer quality) · fixed greedy sample generations.
Runtime (day 2): 164 s train · 375 steps · Modal L4 @ $0.80/hr → ~$0.049. Cumulative study spend to date: ~$0.16 (teacher $0).
