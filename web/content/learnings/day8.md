# Training Feedback — Day 8

**Round:** 8,000 pairs · ppl **7.12** · retention **13.10 (+15.4%)** · judge **1.60** · $0.158

> Testing Day 7's hypothesis: signal or noise?

---

## Finding 1 — The bump HELD: judge 1.60 → 1.60

Day 7's jump did **not** immediately collapse. Two consecutive rounds at **1.60** — the joint-highest of
the study. This is the strongest challenge the plateau conclusion has faced.

**Honest assessment of what this does and doesn't mean:**

- **Does:** rules out "Day 7 was a single-round spike." A two-round hold is harder to dismiss.
- **Doesn't:** establish a *trend*. The judge **did not continue climbing** — it flat-lined at the new
  level. A real capability gain driven by data would be expected to keep improving as data keeps
  growing (another +1,000 pairs arrived this round and bought **+0.00**).

**Sharper framing:** we now have two plateaus — one at ~1.50 (Days 2–6) and possibly one at ~1.60
(Days 7–8). Either the judge stepped to a new flat level, or we are watching a slow random walk on a
noisy 50-question metric. **Day 9 is the deciding round:** continued climb ⇒ real; collapse toward
~1.54 ⇒ the whole 1.46–1.60 range is one noise band around ~1.5.

## Finding 2 — Still no corroborating evidence anywhere else

- Probe unchanged: "…is the preponderance of the evidence" ✓ (identical to Days 5–7).
- No qualitative shift in the other samples (10-K summary still echoes; severability still circular).
- Retention **13.00 → 13.10 (+15.4%)** — monotonic, unaffected.

A genuine quality gain that shows up in *no* sample and in *no* other metric is suspicious. The
absence of corroboration continues to favour the noise reading.

---

**Verdict:** hypothesis still open, leaning noise. Do not revise the conclusion yet — Day 9 decides.
**Note the discipline point:** had we stopped at Day 8, we'd have had a tempting but likely-false
"quality improves after 7k pairs" story. Running the extra rounds is what protects against that.
